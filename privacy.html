Privacy Policy

One Line Journaling · Effective 14 June 2026

One Line Journaling is a private journal. This page says, in plain words, what we keep and what we will never do with it.

What we store

Your email address (to sign you in), your first name, your settings (reminder time, die color), your Pro/subscription status, and your journal — one line and one color per day.

Where it lives

Your journal is stored in our database (hosted on Supabase) and protected in transit and at rest with industry-standard encryption. Server access rules mean your account can only ever read its own lines. Your phone also keeps an offline copy so the app works without a connection.

What we will never do

We don’t sell your data. We don’t show ads. We don’t put trackers in the app. Your words are yours.

Email — no spam, ever

The only email we send is your 6-digit sign-in code. No newsletters, no marketing. If something important changes (like this policy), we’ll tell you inside the app.

Reminders & sharing

The daily reminder is scheduled on your phone itself — not sent from our servers. Share images are created on your phone and handed to your device’s share sheet; we never see or store what you share.

Deleting everything

Settings → Delete account & data permanently erases your account and every line, immediately. No copy is kept.

Children

One Line Journaling is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from them.

Contact

Questions about your privacy? Email onelinejournaling@gmail.com.

If our practices change, we’ll update this page and the effective date above.
